- Calm
- Emotionally intelligent
- Supportive
- Precise
- Grounded

Not gamified.  
Not noisy.  
Not guilt-driven.

# Constraints

- No login
- No cloud

- Local-only storage
- Must genuinely help smokers quit
- Must monetize ethically

We win through:

**Psychology + Design Clarity + Emotional Precision + Retention Engineering**

---

# 🎯 Core Principle

Most quit apps track abstinence.

But smokers relapse because of:

- Identity conflict (“I’m still a smoker”)
- Stress-response conditioning
- Shame spiral after slip
- Environmental cue triggers
- Emotional regulation deficit

So this app must:

1. Replace identity
2. Teach urge tolerance
3. Neutralize shame
4. Replace ritual
5. Make relapse survivable

If it does these five better than competitors, it becomes 10/10.

---

# 🧠 Product Philosophy

Not:
> “You haven’t smoked for 5 days.”

But:
> “You are becoming someone who doesn’t need cigarettes.”

Identity over streak.

That shift is everything.

# 🏁 Onboarding (Critical)

Onboarding must feel like:

> “Finally, something that understands me.”

## Step 1: Choose Your Type

Prompt:
> Which sounds most like you?

- I smoke to deal with stress
- I smoke socially
- I smoke out of boredom
- I smoke automatically
- I’ve tried quitting before

Purpose: psychological mapping.

---

## Step 2: Emotional Awareness

Prompt:
> What do cigarettes give you?

- Calm
- Break
- Focus
- Comfort
- Identity
- Control

Purpose: identify replacement targets.

---

## Step 3: Reframe

Screen message:

> You don’t love cigarettes.  
> You love what they temporarily give you.  
> We’ll build that without nicotine.

---

## Step 4: Define Identity

Instead of:
> When is your quit date?

Ask:
> Are you ready to become a non-smoker today?

Button:
> Yes. Starting now.

No delay.
Immediate identity shift.

# 🔥 Craving Flow (Core Weapon)

When user taps:
“I feel a craving”

Flow must feel emotionally intelligent.

---

## Step 1: Name It

Prompt:
> What triggered this?

Options:
- Stress
- Coffee
- After meal
- Driving
- Social
- Bored
- Withdrawal

---

## Step 2: Normalize

If Stress selected:

> Your brain learned to associate stress with nicotine relief.  
> This is conditioning — not weakness.

Shame reduction is critical.

---

## Step 3: Urge Timer (3 Minutes)

Visual expanding circle.

Text:
> Most cravings peak at 3–5 minutes. Let’s ride this wave.

Slider:
> How strong is it right now?

Track drop in intensity.

This teaches urge surfing (CBT-based).

---

## Step 4: Replacement Ritual

If stress:

> Do this now:

- Inhale 4 seconds
- Hold 4
- Exhale 6
- Repeat 5 times
- Stand up and shake arms for 20 seconds

Replace ritual, don’t suppress it.

---

## Step 5: Identity Reminder

> You’ve handled 12 cravings already.  
> This is who you’re becoming.

Growth framing only.
Never guilt.

---

# 🚨 Relapse Flow (Differentiator)

When user taps:
“I slipped”

Screen:

No red.
No dramatic reset.

Message:

> One cigarette is data, not failure.  
> Let’s understand it.

Prompt:
> What happened?

Select trigger again.

Then:

> Good. Now we know your pattern.  
> The next one will be easier.

---

## Do NOT Reset Streak to Zero

Instead show:

- Longest streak
- % smoke-free days
- Total cigarettes avoided

Activate:

“Recovery Mode” (48 hours extra support notifications)

Purpose:
Prevent shame spiral.
